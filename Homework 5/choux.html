Choux


Restaurant Choux is located on the IJ just behind the station and is housed in an imposing monumental building with plenty of space, high walls and an open kitchen at the back. There, chef Merijn van Berlo watches over his stove. He provides fixed menus in which vegetables play a leading role. His repertoire is rich in original dishes that pleasantly surprise. Al dente roasted beetroot is well served with barbecue-smoked herbs, thinly sliced, smeared with herbs and rolled up resulting in delicious bite, fresh, full-bodied and slightly earthy in taste. In addition, rhubarb gravy and blueberry oil combine to create a filmy mouthfeel and make the whole a feast of flavors. In the same style with a focus on textures, we enjoy a celeriac dumpling filled with fried death trumpet and egg yolk cooked at a low temperature with a beautiful beurre blanc as an elegant acidity. Special wine list with a lot of attention to natural wines from small suppliers and it seems as if the wine list moves with the seasons. In addition, there is a lot of choice from various non-alcoholic kombuchas as good alternative accompaniments to the dishes.
